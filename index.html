01 — Problem

The Form
Asks Too
Much,

Users arrive with intent. The form loses them before they see a single number. Not a product failure — a sequencing failure.

"The form asks for everything before giving anything."
REV

UX
Fixable Drop-off

TRU
Trust-Sensitive Domain
Finance + personal data — users are cautious by default. Every unnecessary field amplifies anxiety.
02 — Audit

6 Issues.
One Root Cause.

Every issue traces back to the same structural problem: asking before giving.

01
Too many fields upfront
All 9 fields shown at once — overwhelming first impression, no progressive structure.
02
No visible progress
No step bar or indicator — users don't know how much effort remains before they quit.
03
Personal data too early
Mobile + email required before any value is shown. The #1 trust barrier at form entry.
04
Poor field grouping
Nominee, language, and contact share the same visual zone — high cognitive load, no clear flow.
05
Weak CTA copy
"Calculate returns" — vague, disconnected, tells users nothing about what comes next.
06
Distracting elements
6 product tabs + promotional content compete with the form for attention mid-task.
03 — User Insight

How Users
Behave in
Financial Flows.

Users scan before committing. They avoid sharing data early. They need instant value to continue — and drop off when effort exceeds perceived benefit.
01 — Behaviour
Scan before committing
They skim the entire form before filling anything. The first impression decides whether they invest effort at all. A wall of 9 fields signals "this will be hard."
02 — Behaviour
Avoid early data sharing
Sharing a phone number before seeing value feels like a trap. It signals the company's interest over theirs — and triggers immediate suspicion.
03 — Behaviour
Need instant value
If they don't see a tangible benefit within Step 1, they leave. No quote = no reason to stay. Value must come before the ask.
04 — Behaviour
Drop off when effort > benefit
The mental cost of completing the form must stay below the perceived reward. The moment it flips — users exit. The redesign tips this balance back.

06 — Trust Design

Trust Is a
Design Decision.

Six targeted enhancements — each one addresses a specific anxiety point in the user journey.

SECURITY
"Your data is secure"
Placed next to fields that trigger the most anxiety — not buried in a footer. Visible on every step.
CONTEXT
Microcopy explains why
Each field has a short hint: "Used to check eligibility." Reduces suspicion and improves data accuracy.
SEQUENCE
Contact after value
Contact details only after the premium estimate is shown. Sharing feels logical — not intrusive.
FOCUS
Noise removed
Product tabs and promotional banners stripped from the form view. Nothing competes with the task.
OPTIONAL
Nominee is optional
Explicitly optional. Can be added post-purchase. Removes a heavy decision from an early stage.
MINIMAL
Calm, focused UI
One question at a time. Soft shadows, no heavy borders. Visual restraint = financial confidence.
